Risk Disclosure

Please read this carefully before trading. Trading carries significant risk and may not be right for you.

Last updated: 8 May 2026 · Effective immediately

In short. Trading financial instruments (FX, indices, crypto-assets, commodities, futures and similar) carries a high level of risk. You can lose some or all of your money, and with leveraged products you can lose more than you put in. Anything RNT Capital shares is for education only. We are not licensed by the MFSA and we do not give personal financial advice. Past performance does not guarantee future results. If in doubt, get independent financial advice before you trade.

1. Trading involves significant risk

The instruments we discuss and share educational examples about are volatile. That includes spot foreign exchange (FX), stock indices (e.g. S&P 500, Nasdaq, FTSE), crypto-assets (e.g. BTC, ETH), commodities (e.g. gold, oil), and futures. Prices can move sharply against you, sometimes within seconds, especially around news events, low-liquidity periods, or unexpected geopolitical developments.

Trading these instruments may not be suitable for all investors. Whether it's appropriate for you depends on your financial situation, your knowledge and experience, your risk tolerance, your investment objectives, and your ability to bear losses. You should only trade with money you can genuinely afford to lose.

2. You can lose more than you invest

Many of the instruments above are commonly traded with leverage (also called margin). Leverage amplifies both gains and losses. With leveraged products such as margined FX, CFDs, or futures, your losses can exceed your initial deposit. Potentially by a very large multiple. You may be required to deposit additional funds at short notice (a "margin call") or have your positions closed out at a loss without further reference to you.

Crypto-assets specifically are highly volatile and largely unregulated as financial instruments. Their value can fall dramatically (including to zero), and you may not be able to sell when you want to.

Everything we publish (on this website, on Instagram, in emails, and inside the members' community) is provided for educational and informational purposes only. This includes:

  • Trade setups, signals, and entry/exit ideas;
  • Market reads, structure breakdowns, and chart annotations;
  • Lessons on risk management, psychology, journaling, and other topics;
  • Examples of past trades, win-rates, or account performance.

Nothing of the above is, or should be construed as:

  • Personal investment advice tailored to your situation;
  • An offer or solicitation to buy, sell, or hold any specific financial instrument;
  • A recommendation that any particular trade is suitable for you.

We do not know your individual financial circumstances, risk profile, or investment goals. Any decision you make based on our content is your own decision, taken at your own risk.

5. Past performance is not a guide to future results

Any reference to past trades, hypothetical results, win-rates, or account performance is for illustration only. That goes for our trades and any member's. Past performance is not indicative or a guarantee of future results. Markets change, and a strategy that worked in the past may not work in the future. Hypothetical or back-tested performance has inherent limitations: it does not involve real money, may not account for slippage, fees, taxes, or psychological factors, and is generally not a reliable indicator of what real trading would have produced.

We do not guarantee:

  • That any signal or setup we share will be profitable;

  • That you will become a profitable trader as a result of joining the community.

Trading is a difficult, competitive activity in which the majority of retail participants lose money. We are honest about this. Discipline improves your odds, but it does not eliminate the risk of loss.

If you are uncertain whether trading is appropriate for your circumstances. Or before acting on anything we share. You should seek independent financial advice from a properly authorised and regulated financial advisor in your country of residence. They can take into account your specific situation in a way we cannot.

The tax treatment of trading gains and losses depends on your individual circumstances and the country in which you are resident. We do not provide tax advice. You are responsible for understanding and complying with the tax rules that apply to you, and we recommend consulting a qualified tax professional if you are unsure.
