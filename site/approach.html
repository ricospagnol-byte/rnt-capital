Our approach

How we actually trade.

Trend first, then range, then reaction. Indicators come last — they just confirm what price has already shown you.

01
Trend

Start with the bigger picture.

Before any trade, we look at structure across multiple timeframes. The higher timeframe is the boss — everything else has to fit around it. No exceptions, no "but this looks good on the 5-minute."

02
Range

Find where the market actually cares.

Highs, lows, the prices price keeps coming back to. Where the market has reacted before is usually where it'll react again. We mark these levels by hand every week — no shortcuts.

03
Reaction

Let the market show its hand first.

Patience does most of the work. We don't try to predict — we wait. When price hits a level, how it reacts (speed, volume, structure) tells you what's actually going on. The level itself is just a meeting point.

04
Risk

Work out your size before you click anything.

Position sizing isn't a slider you fiddle with after spotting a setup. We decide what we're risking before we even look at entry — fixed % per trade, a cap on total exposure, and a hard daily loss limit. Boring. Non-negotiable.

In practice

What we do — and don't.

WE DO
  • · Post setups and signals with the full reasoning.
  • · Map structure top-down, every week.
  • · Pre-plan entries, stops, and targets.
  • · Size every trade by risk-per-trade %.
  • · Journal every position — wins and losses.
  • · Review each other's trades, honestly.
WE DON'T
  • · Send blind alerts with no reasoning.
  • · Trade against higher-timeframe structure.
  • · Move stops to "give it room."
  • · Size up after losses to "win it back."
  • · Care what some random on the internet is doing.
In one line

Indicators aren't the answer. Structure, levels, and how price reacts to them — that's the answer.
